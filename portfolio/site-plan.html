Introduction

Welcome to my Occupational Safety Professional Portfolio website! As a dedicated professional committed to ensuring workplace safety and well-being, I am thrilled to showcase my journey, expertise, and accomplishments in the field of occupational safety. With a passion for safeguarding lives and promoting a culture of health and safety, I have acquired a diverse skill set and hands-on experience that I am eager to share. From risk assessments and hazard mitigation strategies to training programs and regulatory compliance, my portfolio reflects my dedication to creating safer and healthier work environments for all.

Skills

Throughout attaining my degree in Occupational Safety I have acquired the following skills: 1. Risk Assessment: Identifying potential hazards and assessing their likelihood and severity in the workplace. 2. Hazard Identification and Mitigation: Recognizing various types of hazards and implementing effective controls to minimize risks. 3. Safety Compliance: Understanding and adhering to relevant occupational health and safety regulations, standards, and best practices. 4. Incident Investigation: Conducting thorough investigations into workplace incidents, accidents, or near misses to determine root causes and prevent recurrence. 5. Safety Training and Education: Developing and delivering training programs to educate employees on safety procedures, protocols, and practices. 6. Emergency Preparedness and Response: Developing emergency response plans, conducting drills, and coordinating responses to workplace emergencies such as fires, chemical spills, or medical incidents. 7. Ergonomics Assessment: Assessing ergonomic factors in the workplace to optimize workstation design and prevent musculoskeletal disorders. 8. Industrial Hygiene Monitoring: Conducting air quality assessments, noise monitoring, and exposure assessments to protect workers from occupational health hazards. 9. Job Safety Analysis (JSA): Analyzing job tasks and identifying potential hazards to develop safe work procedures and protocols. 10. Safety Management Systems (SMS): Implementing and maintaining comprehensive safety management systems to continually improve safety performance within an organization. 11. Behavior-Based Safety: Implementing programs to promote safe behaviors among employees and foster a positive safety culture. 12. Safety Auditing and Inspection: Conducting regular audits and inspections to identify safety deficiencies and ensure compliance with safety regulations and standards. 13. Incident Reporting and Recordkeeping: Establishing systems for reporting and recording workplace incidents, injuries, and illnesses for regulatory compliance and continuous improvement. 14. Communication and Collaboration: Effectively communicating safety policies, procedures, and initiatives across all levels of an organization and collaborating with stakeholders to achieve safety goals. 15. Crisis Management: Developing contingency plans and protocols to manage crises and unforeseen events that may impact workplace safety.

Experience

In preperation for entering the workforce I have been involved in several safety related jobs and activities: 1. Inspecting and refilling first aid kits to ensure they have the proper materials. 2. Moving chemical satelites when they reach their fill lines to the hazardous waste facility. 3. Training individuals on proper first aid and cpr techniques. 4. Filling out worker compensation forms when accidents happen in the workplace. 5. Examining and testing fume hoods to ensure they are working as they should be. 6. Examining and testing eye wash stations and bottles, ensuring they are up to date on maintenance. 7. Inspecting and measuring rooms to ensure they meet fire saftey codes for usable space. 8. Building testing plans for chemical exposures, and using monitoring devices to detemermine the amount and source of the chemical. 9. Completing safety inspection forms to ensure construction sites are up to code in OSHA guidelines. 10. Picking up flourescent bulbs and crushing them to be disposed of properly. 11. Picking up, transporting, and disposing of electrical waste. 12. Picking up, transporting, and disposing of chemical waste. 13. Participating in fire watch, including watching the fire panel, and surveying events. 14. Inpecting and retagging fire extinguishers to ensure that they are working properly. 15. Testing fire alarms and smoke detectors to ensure they are working properly.
